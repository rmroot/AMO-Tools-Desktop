Total energy consumed by the plant.

ANNUAL THERMAL DEMAND

Annual fuel consumption.

CHP AVAILABILITY

The percentage of time that a CHP system is either up and running or available for use.
Typical range: 90-98%.

Units: %

THERMAL UTILIZATION

Amount of available thermal energy captured and used for CHP Generation.
Typical range: 80-100%

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

DISPLACED THERMAL EFFICIENCY

Efficiency of the equipment currently providing thermal energy to the facility. This can be boilers producing steam or hot water, or direct processes heaters.
Typical range: 75-82%

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

BOILER/THERMAL FUEL COSTS

Cost of fuel currently used to produce thermal energy at the facility.

Units: $/MMBtu

AVERAGE ELECTRICITY COST

Total price of electricity at the facility. To calculate this value, take the total bill amount for a representative month and divide by the energy consumed (kWh).

Units: $/kWh

CHP FUEL COSTS

In most cases, CHP fuel costs will be same as the existing fuel costs. However, in certain states, fuel for CHP Systems is provided at a special rate. If your state/utility offers a discounted CHP fuel price enter it in this subsection else enter the same fuel price as existing system.

Units: $/MMBtu

CHP FUEL COSTS

Cost of fuel currently used to produce thermal energy at the facility.

Units: $/MMBtu

STANDBY RATE

Standby rates are charges levied by utilities when an on-site CHP system, experiences a scheduled or emergency outage.

Units: $/kW

PERCENT AVERAGE PER kWh ELECTRIC COST AVOIDED

Percent of average electricity costs per kWh saved by one kWh of CHP electricity.
Typical range: 70-95%

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100
